# DietPlanner Privacy Policy

**Last updated: May 10, 2026**

## Overview

DietPlanner ("the App") is a nutrition planning application built for Android and Solana Mobile users. Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains what data is processed, where it is stored, and rights that may apply under GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and similar laws worldwide.

## Data We Collect

### Data Stored Locally on Your Device
- **Profile information**: Age, sex, height, weight, activity level, and dietary goals you enter into the App.
- **Preferences**: Selected interests, meal count, and nutrition goals.
- **Entitlements**: Records of premium features you have purchased.
- **Coach data**: Daily check-in status and streak count.
- **Legal acceptance**: Whether you have accepted the Terms, Privacy Policy, health disclaimer, and payment disclosures.

All of the above data is stored **exclusively on your device** using secure local storage such as Expo SecureStore. **We do not transmit, collect, or store this local profile data on DietPlanner servers.**

### Data We Do NOT Collect
- **Wallet addresses or private keys**: Your Solana wallet connection is handled entirely by the Mobile Wallet Adapter protocol. DietPlanner never has access to your private keys, seed phrases, or wallet credentials.
- **Private transaction history database**: Payment transactions are processed on the Solana blockchain and transaction details are public by blockchain design. DietPlanner does not operate a private analytics database of your transactions.
- **Location data**: The App does not request or use your location.
- **Analytics or tracking**: The App does not include any analytics SDKs, tracking pixels, or telemetry.
- **Personal identifiers**: We do not collect your name, email, phone number, or any other personally identifiable information.

## Blockchain Transactions

When you purchase premium features, the transaction occurs on the Solana blockchain:
- The transaction is between your wallet and the DietPlanner treasury wallet.
- Transaction details (wallet addresses, amounts, timestamps) are publicly visible on the Solana blockchain, as is the nature of all public blockchain transactions.
- DietPlanner has no ability to reverse, modify, or access funds in your wallet.

## Legal Bases and Worldwide Rights

Where privacy laws such as GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, or similar laws apply, local app processing is generally based on user consent and performance of requested app functionality. Blockchain payment processing is necessary to complete the transaction requested by the user. Users may clear local app data or uninstall the App to remove local data. Public blockchain data cannot be deleted by DietPlanner.

## Notifications

If you grant notification permissions, the App schedules local notifications on your device for:
- Meal time reminders
- Micro-Coach expiry warnings
- Daily check-in nudges

These notifications are **entirely local** — no data is sent to any server to deliver them.

## Third-Party Services

The App connects to the following third-party services:
- **Solana RPC endpoints** (Helius, public mainnet-beta): To query blockchain state for token balances and submit transactions. Only your public wallet address is sent to these endpoints.
- **Mobile Wallet Adapter**: To interact with your Solana wallet app. No data passes through DietPlanner — communication is direct between the MWA protocol and your wallet.

## Data Retention and Deletion

All data is stored locally on your device. To delete all DietPlanner data:
1. Clear the App's data through your device's Settings > Apps > DietPlanner > Clear Data, or
2. Uninstall the App.

There is no server-side data to delete because we do not collect any.

## Children's Privacy

DietPlanner is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect data from minors.

## Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be indicated by the "Last updated" date at the top. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
